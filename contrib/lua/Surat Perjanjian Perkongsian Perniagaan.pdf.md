
 
# How to Write a Business Partnership Agreement in Malaysia
 
A business partnership agreement is a legal document that outlines the rights and responsibilities of the partners in a business venture. It also defines the terms and conditions of the partnership, such as the profit-sharing ratio, the decision-making process, the dissolution procedure, and the dispute resolution mechanism. A business partnership agreement is essential for any business that involves more than one person, as it can help to avoid conflicts, misunderstandings, and legal issues in the future.

In Malaysia, a business partnership agreement can be written in Malay or English, and it can be either oral or written. However, it is advisable to have a written agreement that is signed by all the partners and stamped by the Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia (LHDN). A written agreement can provide more clarity and certainty for the partners, as well as serve as evidence in case of any disputes or litigation.
